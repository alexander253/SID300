Distinctive Features

The Brush Tailed Rabbit Rat (try saying that fast 5 times), or simply known as the Rabbit-Rat, are often the same size as a pet household rabbit weighing in at about 150 grams. They might seem big for a rat, but they are smaller than their other close relatives.

They have deep black eyes which are hard not to notice.

Their most distinctive feature is their long brush-tipped tail and relativley big ears.

Diet

The main diet of the Rabbit-Rat consits of plants which make them a herbivore.
